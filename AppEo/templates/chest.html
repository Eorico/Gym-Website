Chest Workout

Master your chest exercises with these effective workouts

Top Chest Exercises

Bench Press

The bench press is a classic chest exercise that targets the pectoralis major. It's great for building upper body strength and mass.
Dumbbell Chest Press

This exercise uses dumbbells to engage the chest muscles while also providing a greater range of motion compared to the barbell press.
Push-ups

Push-ups are a bodyweight exercise that targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps. They are a great way to build strength with no equipment needed.
Chest Dips

Chest dips are a compound movement that work the chest, shoulders, and triceps. They are great for building strength and definition.
Incline Bench Press

The incline bench press targets the upper chest and shoulders. This variation is great for adding size and definition to the upper chest region.
Chest Flyes

Chest flyes involve bringing the arms together in a wide arc, targeting the chest muscles. This isolation exercise is great for sculpting the chest.

Chest Workout Details

Beginner Workout:

3 sets of 12 repetitions
NOTE: Focus on learning proper form and control. Use lighter weights to build endurance and technique.

Intermediate Workout:

4 sets of 8-10 repetitions
NOTE: Increase weight slightly to challenge strength.
Maintain proper form and gradually reduce rest times between sets.

Veteran Workout:

5 sets of 5-6 repetitions
NOTE: Use heavier weights to focus on maximum strength.
Increase intensity with shorter rest intervals.
Include techniques like pause reps (pausing at the bottom of the lift) for greater time under tension.
