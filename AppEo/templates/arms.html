Arm Workout

Build your arms with these effective biceps, triceps, and forearm exercises

Top Arm Exercises

Bicep Curls

Bicep curls are a fundamental exercise for building arm strength and size. They target the biceps brachii and forearms, helping to develop your upper arms.
Tricep Dips

Tricep dips are a bodyweight exercise that targets the triceps, helping to define and strengthen the back of the upper arm.
Hammer Curls

Hammer curls are a variation of the bicep curl that also target the forearms, building both the biceps and forearm strength.
Tricep Pushdowns

Tricep pushdowns are performed using a cable machine, targeting the triceps with a focus on the long head of the muscle.
Close-grip Bench Press

The close-grip bench press is a variation of the traditional bench press that targets the triceps more than the chest.
Preacher Curls

Preacher curls are an excellent exercise for isolating the biceps. They help prevent swinging and focus on the muscle's contraction.

Arms Workout Details

Beginner Workout:

3 sets of 12 repetitions
NOTE: Focus on learning proper form and control. Use lighter weights to build endurance and technique.

Intermediate Workout:

4 sets of 8-10 repetitions
NOTE: Increase weight slightly to challenge strength.
Maintain proper form and gradually reduce rest times between sets.

Veteran Workout:

5 sets of 5-6 repetitions
NOTE: Use heavier weights to focus on maximum strength.
Increase intensity with shorter rest intervals.
Include techniques like pause reps (pausing at the bottom of the lift) for greater time under tension.
